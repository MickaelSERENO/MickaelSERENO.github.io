Experiences

PostDoc at INRIA

PostDoc supervised by Jean-Daniel Fekete at INRIA Saclay, team AVIZ. Jan 2022 - Dec 2023.

The PostDoc consisted on visualizing the care pathways of patients from the medical records the CNAM (French social security) stores. This data contains all the medical records of all the french citizens for reimbursement purposes. With data mining, one can derive a care pathway for a given disease a patient underwent in order to get cured. As some diseases touch a large part of the population and need several medical acts for one to get cured, the exploratory system may not have enough computing power to remain responsive. With Jean-Daniel, we investigated with our prototype, ParcoursVis (see also the gitlab repository), the progressive data visualization paradigm. This paradigm allows the system to remain responsive by yielding intermediate results upon which the domain expert user can work on while the system refines the results at later stages.

End-of-study internship at Safran Electronics & Defense

Done at Eragny-Sur-Oise, France from March 2018 to August 2018.

End of study internship. Safran Electronics & Defense is developing a library for 3D map visualization based on geographic datasets. They can visualize multi types of images mapped into a 3D ground, like satellite images, topography maps, drone video camera streams, and add 3D items to better understand the map they are visualizing.
My mission was to provide Virtual Reality functionalities into the existing library. I have to propose manipulation paradigms and resolve visualization issues that VR headsets introduce, especially the Oculus DK2 they gave me. At the end, I had to create a proof-of-concept application where users can navigate and visualize this 3D map with Safan specific information.

Intern at ISMO (CNRS)

Internship at ISMO, 1 month, summer 2015. Work published at IISC-21.

 I have made my second year internship at ISMO, where my mission was to provide a solution to align a sample surface normal to its rotation axis while making an atom diffraction in an Ultra Hight Vacuum (UHV) system. Because of the mechanics, we couldn't have done it with the usual solution by hands. We had to compute the tilt angle between these two axis, and correct the angle only with rotations programmed. The solution was made with an Arduino, programmed in C/C++.
